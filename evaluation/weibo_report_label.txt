WITHIN-WEIBO: RUMOUR vs NON-RUMOUR STRUCTURAL ANALYSIS
Factual interpretation of empirical results
=======================================================

SAMPLE
------
Rumour cascades:     2,278
Non-rumour cascades: 2,249
Unlabelled (excluded): 132
Labels are balanced — no class imbalance problem.


RAW NUMBERS
-----------
Metric              Rumour      Non-rumour    Difference
----------          ------      ----------    ----------
Mean depth           3.914        6.724        -41.8%
Mean width         566.647      494.695        +14.5%
Mean d/w ratio       0.022        0.090        -75.6%
Mean size          721.201      876.169        -17.7%


WHAT EACH NUMBER MEANS
-----------------------

1. RUMOURS ARE SHALLOWER (-41.8% depth, 3.9 vs 6.7 hops)

   A rumour on Weibo travels on average 3.9 hops from the root post.
   A non-rumour travels 6.7 hops. Rumours stop chaining sooner.

   This means people repost rumours quickly and broadly but do not
   continue to engage with them in long sequences. The cascade burns
   wide and fast, then dies. Non-rumours sustain longer chains —
   people keep replying, discussing, and forwarding in threads.

2. RUMOURS ARE WIDER (+14.5% width, 567 vs 495 nodes at peak level)

   At the busiest level of the cascade, rumours have 567 nodes on
   average versus 495 for non-rumours. Rumours spread to more people
   simultaneously at each hop — a classic viral burst pattern.

3. THE D/W RATIO GAP IS THE STRONGEST SIGNAL (-75.6%, 0.022 vs 0.090)

   This is the most important number. Rumours have a depth/width ratio
   of 0.022. Non-rumours have 0.090 — more than four times higher.

   In plain terms: relative to how wide a cascade spreads, non-rumours
   travel four times deeper. Rumours are extremely flat relative to
   their breadth. Non-rumours, while still wide, have proportionally
   much more vertical structure.

   This ratio is a single number that captures the structural shape of
   a cascade. A classifier that uses this feature will have a real
   signal to work with, not noise.

4. NON-RUMOURS ARE LARGER OVERALL (-17.7% size, 721 vs 876 nodes)

   Non-rumour cascades have 876 nodes on average; rumour cascades have
   721. Non-rumours generate more total engagement. This fits with the
   depth finding: non-rumours sustain longer discussion, accumulating
   more nodes across more levels.


THE CORE FINDING
----------------
Rumours on Weibo spread as wide bursts — many reposts at each level,
but chains terminate quickly. Non-rumours generate sustained discussion
— they go deeper and accumulate more total engagement even if each
individual level is slightly narrower.

This is not the same pattern in every dataset. On Twitter-15 the
distinction between true and false content is less structurally clean
because Twitter's reply threading creates different tree shapes than
Weibo's repost mechanism. The Weibo-specific pattern here is genuine
and large enough to be useful.


WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE GNN
----------------------------
Cascade structure IS a discriminating signal within Weibo — not just
between communities but between labels within the same community.

The depth/width ratio (0.022 rumour vs 0.090 non-rumour, a 4x gap) is
large enough that a GNN encoding structural position — depth, breadth,
level normalisation — will pick this up during training without any
text features at all. The is_root flag and level-normalised structural
features in the node feature matrix directly capture this.

Practically: the b- matrix (misinformation spreading) should encode
a low-depth, high-width prior for Weibo. The b+ matrix (credible
content) should encode a higher-depth, moderate-width prior. These
are different from the Twitter-15 priors and different from each other
within Weibo — both facts are now empirically established.


WHAT TO BE CAREFUL ABOUT
--------------------------
The 132 unlabelled events were excluded from this analysis. If those
events are structurally unusual (e.g. removed posts that spread very
far before deletion), the true rumour structural profile could be
more extreme than the 2,278-event sample suggests. This is worth
checking before claiming the d/w ratio gap will hold in production.
