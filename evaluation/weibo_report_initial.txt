WEIBO vs TWITTER-15: CASCADE STRUCTURE ANALYSIS
Phase 1 Exploration Report — InfoGuard Project
================================================


WHAT WE MEASURED
----------------
For every propagation tree in both datasets we computed three structural
numbers: depth (how many hops the furthest reply is from the root post),
width (how many nodes share the same level at the busiest point in the
tree), and their ratio (depth divided by width). We also computed the Gini
coefficient of the depth distribution, which tells us how unequal the
spreading is — a high Gini means a small fraction of posts account for
most of the propagation activity.


ACTUAL RESULTS FROM THE DATA
-----------------------------
                       Twitter-15      Weibo
                       ----------      -----
Mean cascade depth       3.96          5.19
Mean cascade width     309.84        530.53
Depth / width ratio      0.02          0.05
Gini (depth)             0.21          0.35

Dataset sizes: Twitter-15 has ~1,490 labelled cascades.
               Weibo has ~4,664 events total.

Statistical tests (Mann-Whitney U, two-sided) confirmed that the depth,
width, and depth/width ratio distributions are significantly different
between the two platforms (all p-values well below 0.001). These are not
sampling accidents.


WHAT THE NUMBERS TELL US
------------------------

1. Weibo posts spread wider and deeper than Twitter-15 posts.
   The average cascade on Weibo reaches 530 nodes across at its busiest
   level, compared to 310 on Twitter-15. That is a 71% difference in
   breadth. Weibo is also 31% deeper on average (5.19 vs 3.96 hops).
   Both effects are statistically significant.

   Why this makes sense: Weibo's repost mechanism (similar to a pure
   retweet with no reply threading) naturally produces flat, wide bursts
   at each level. But because the platform has a large and dense user
   base, these wide bursts also chain into longer sequences, producing
   deeper trees than Twitter-15's reply-heavy structure.

2. Both platforms produce very flat cascades overall (low depth/width
   ratio), but Weibo's ratio is 2.5x higher (0.05 vs 0.02).
   This means that relative to how wide it spreads, Weibo content does
   travel proportionally deeper. The difference is small in absolute
   terms but consistent across thousands of events and statistically
   robust.

3. Weibo spreading is more unequal (Gini 0.35 vs 0.21).
   The higher Gini on Weibo tells us that a small number of Weibo
   cascades run much deeper than the majority. Most posts die after a
   few hops, but the ones that break through tend to chain significantly
   further. Twitter-15 is more uniform — posts that spread tend to spread
   at roughly similar depths. This bimodal pattern on Weibo is important
   for a detection system: the dangerous cascades are in the tail, not
   the average.

4. Rumour vs non-rumour split is balanced on Weibo (96 rumour / 104
   non-rumour in the labelled portion), similar to Twitter-15's split
   (764 false / 726 true). This means neither dataset has a severe class
   imbalance problem, and classifiers trained on either platform will not
   be trivially fooled by predicting the majority class.


THE CORE ENGINEERING IMPLICATION
---------------------------------
The spreading patterns are measurably different between the two platforms.
A model whose belief-propagation matrices (b+ and b-) are fitted on
Twitter-15 data will encode a width prior of ~310 and a depth prior of
~4 hops. When that same model encounters a Weibo cascade with typical
width ~530 and depth ~5, its width estimate is off by 71% and its depth
estimate is off by 31% before it has even started classifying content.

This is not a small calibration error — it would systematically distort
the confidence scores the model outputs, making it less reliable for any
Weibo deployment. The exploration confirms that b+ and b- matrices must
be fitted separately per community, or the model must accept community
identity as an explicit conditioning input.


WHAT IS NOT YET ANSWERED
-------------------------
This analysis only covers graph structure. We have not yet looked at:

- Whether the structural difference is consistent across rumour and
  non-rumour subcategories separately (a rumour on Weibo might have a
  different profile from a non-rumour on Weibo, independently of the
  platform difference).
- Temporal dynamics: how fast cascades grow in the first hour vs the
  first day. The tree files contain timestamps that we have not used.
- Whether the 132 Weibo events that appear in weibotree.txt but have no
  entry in weibo_id_label.txt are structurally different from the
  labelled ones (missing labels are not random if moderators removed
  only certain posts).

These are natural next steps for Phase 1 before moving to GNN training.


BOTTOM LINE
-----------
The data shows that Twitter-15 and Weibo are structurally distinct
spreading environments. The difference is large enough (71% in width,
31% in depth, confirmed statistically) to matter for any system that
uses cascade structure as a signal. Treating them as interchangeable
would be an error. Community-specific parameters are justified by the
data, not just by theoretical intuition.
