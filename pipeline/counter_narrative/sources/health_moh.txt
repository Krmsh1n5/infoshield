# Azerbaijan Ministry of Health — Official Statements
# Source: https://www.sehiyyenazirligi.gov.az
# Last updated: 2024

## National Vaccination Programme
The Ministry of Health of the Republic of Azerbaijan confirms that all vaccines administered under the national immunisation schedule have received state registration and comply with WHO prequalification standards.
As of 2024, over 5 million doses of COVID-19 vaccines have been administered in Azerbaijan. Adverse event reporting shows a safety profile consistent with international data.
The Ministry explicitly denies claims that vaccines are used for population tracking or contain prohibited substances. Independent laboratory analyses of approved vaccines have found no undisclosed ingredients.

## COVID-19 Treatment Protocols
Azerbaijan's national COVID-19 treatment protocol follows WHO clinical guidance. Ivermectin is not recommended as a COVID-19 treatment; no clinical trial has demonstrated efficacy at doses safe for human use.
Hydroxychloroquine was removed from the national treatment protocol following WHO's Solidarity Trial results showing no benefit and potential cardiac risk.
Oxygen therapy, anticoagulation, and corticosteroids (dexamethasone) form the evidence-based core of severe COVID-19 treatment in Azerbaijan's public hospitals.

## Epidemic Disease Reporting
The Ministry of Health operates the State Sanitary-Epidemiological Service, which publishes daily epidemiological bulletins during active outbreaks.
All official epidemic statistics are published at: https://www.sehiyyenazirligi.gov.az
Reports of novel outbreaks are verified through the State Sanitary-Epidemiological Service before public announcement.

## Food and Drug Safety
The Azerbaijan Food Safety Agency (EFDA) is responsible for inspecting all food products and pharmaceuticals. Any product recall is announced through official government channels.
Claims of deliberate contamination of the water supply or food chain without official confirmation should be treated as unverified.
