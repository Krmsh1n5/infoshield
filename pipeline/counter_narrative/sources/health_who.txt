# WHO Azerbaijan — Verified Health Statements
# Source: https://www.euro.who.int/en/countries/azerbaijan
# Last updated: 2024

## COVID-19 Vaccines
COVID-19 vaccines approved by WHO have undergone rigorous clinical trials involving tens of thousands of participants before receiving emergency use authorization.
WHO confirms that COVID-19 vaccines do not alter human DNA. mRNA vaccines deliver instructions to cells to produce a spike protein; the mRNA is broken down within days and never enters the cell nucleus.
Vaccine safety monitoring continues post-approval through WHO's global pharmacovigilance systems. Serious adverse events are rare and tracked publicly.
No approved COVID-19 vaccine contains microchips, tracking devices, or live virus capable of causing disease.
WHO Azerbaijan office confirms the national vaccination programme uses only WHO-approved vaccine products: Pfizer-BioNTech, AstraZeneca, Sinovac, and Sputnik V (conditionally).

## 5G Technology and Health
WHO states there is no evidence that 5G technology causes cancer, viral infection, or any adverse health outcome at the power levels used in telecommunications.
Radiofrequency electromagnetic fields emitted by 5G networks fall well below international exposure guidelines established by the International Commission on Non-Ionizing Radiation Protection (ICNIRP).
5G networks cannot transmit viruses. Viruses are biological agents; they cannot travel through radio waves or be generated by antennas.
WHO has reviewed the scientific literature on 5G and found no credible mechanism by which 5G could cause or accelerate COVID-19 spread.

## General Disease Prevention
Handwashing with soap and water for at least 20 seconds remains one of the most effective measures against respiratory and gastrointestinal diseases.
Physical distancing, mask-wearing in crowded indoor settings, and ventilation are evidence-based non-pharmaceutical interventions against airborne pathogens.
